App Assist

The App Assist feature gives you the ability to monitor how the apps downloaded to your phone affect your privacy and security. By knowing which apps can access your personal information, messages, pictures, location, or consume a lot of battery, you can make more informed decisions on which apps you keep on your phone.

Device access scoring App Assist analyzes a variety of metrics to provide a Device Access Score for each app installed on your phone. These metrics include potential risks such as access to your personal information, your location, and high battery/resource utilization. App Assist displays a scoring bar that indicates whether an app has low device access (few bars) or high device access (many bars).

View, sort and filter app information App Assist allows you to review your apps by device access type or by app category (Games, Social Networking Apps, Navigation Apps, or Other Apps). The following describes the access types:.

  • Personal Info : - Apps in this category can access your personal information, including your phone number, your contacts, and your calendar.
  • Messages or Pictures :- Apps in this category can access your text messages, emails, camera, and microphone.
  • Location: - Apps in this category can check your phone location.
  • Battery: Apps in this category have high resource usage..

Remove unwanted apps

To remove apps from your phone:

  1. Launch Asurion LABS Application on your phone.
  2. On the main menu, press the App Assist button.
  3. Locate the app you want to uninstall by browsing the proper risk category or app type.
  4. Press the Uninstall button next to the app name to remove it from your phone.

Flag apps for device access concern

If you have an app installed on your phone that you feel is a risk to your privacy, you may flag the app through the App Assist feature. This information will be captured in our database and will be used in the future for even greater security.
To flag an app for access concern:

  1. Launch Asurion LABS Application on your phone.
  2. On the main menu, press the App Assist button.
  3. Locate the app you want to flag by browsing for access type or app category.
  4. In the app details, press the Flag App For Access Concern button.
  5. On the next screen you can provide additional information about your concern.
  6. Once complete, a red flag icon is displayed in the app list and the app details view, indicating that the app has been flagged.
  7. On the next screen, you can provide additional information about your privacy concern. Once complete, you will notice the app has been flagged by the appearance of a red flag icon displayed in both the app list and details vi ews. You can also Unflag Device Access Concern by repeating the steps outlined above.
