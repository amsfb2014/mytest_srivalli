How do I find a lost phone? There are two options for finding a lost phone:

To find a misplaced phone nearby, sound a phone alarm:

  1. Log in to LABS QA.
  2. Click the Sound Alarm button at the top of the page.
  3. In the popup window, click the Sound Alarm button.
  4. To disarm the alarm, press Stop Alarm button on the phone or press the volume on the side of your phone up or down.

To find a lost or stolen phone using GPS technology:

  1. Log in to LABS QA.
  2. Click the Locate Phone button at the top of the Location page.
  3. In the popup window, click the Locate Phone button.
  4. The phone location will be updated on the Location page.

Remember:Safety first. Asurion recommends you do not attempt to retrieve your phone from any location that is not safe, that you do not recognize, or that is not familiar to you. If you believe your phone has been stolen or is in an unsafe or unfamiliar location, you should contact your local law enforcement.

How can I improve location accuracy? Location accuracy is determined by a number of factors, including things like proximity to a cell tower, signal strength, and the phone’s current location (GPS reading are more accurate if your phone is outdoors). To ensure the most accurate locations possible, make sure all Location Services (GPS, etc.) settings on your phone are set to ON.

To check GPS settings:

  1. Launch the Asurion LABS Application on your phone.
  2. On the main menu, press the Location button.
  3. At the top of the screen, icons indicate whether each location setting is ON (green checkmark) or OFF (warning icon). You can click any Location Services setting to turn it ON or OFF.

What are the security settings?

Your security settings include the following:

  1. Auto Threat Scan: Allows you to set a custom schedule for automatic threat scans on your phone.
  2. Real Time Scans: When On, the Asurion LABS Application scans apps and files for threats as they are downloaded/installed.
  3. Automatic Media Scans: When On, the Application automatically scans the SD card for threats whenever it is changed.
  4. Tray Notifications: Android users can receive security notifications in the toolbar.
  5. Cloud A/V: Anonymously shares your security activity to help improve mobile security.
  6. Delete Notification: When On, an optional feedback form is displayed for sharing information with our security research team about why you deleted the Application.
  7. Safe Browsing: Will protect your phone and privacy from potential threats while you surf the Web from your phone.
  8. Airplane Mode Lock: When On, requires a password to enable Airplane Mode on your phone, which provides added security by preventing features such as Lock and Locate from being performed.
  9. Reset Whitelist: This option will clear the list of any threats you have ignored in the past.

What is App Assist? App Assist gives you the ability to monitor how the apps downloaded to your phone affect your privacy and security. By knowing which apps can access your personal information, messages, pictures, location, or consume a lot of battery, you can make more informed decisions on which apps you keep on your phone.

[Back to Top]

What is a Device Access Score? App Assist analyzes a variety of metrics to provide a Device Access Score for each app installed on your phone. These metrics include potential risks such as access to your personal information, your location, and high battery/resource utilization. App Assist displays a scoring bar that indicates whether an app has low device access (few bars) or high device access (many bars).

[Back to Top]

What can I do with the information that App Assist provides? App Assist looks at permissions granted to installed applications on your phone, and monitors their actual behavior. App Assist allows you to:

  1. Learn – Better understand what privacy aspects downloaded apps and media files can access on your phone. (For example, a free game might send your location, EMEI, SIM card ID, address book, or phone number to a remote server, as you play the game.)
  2. Empower – Help you decide whether or not you want to use an app based on analytic insight and comparison to other apps.
  3. Protect – Take action against apps that otherwise go undetected by anti-virus software, but may be accessing inappropriate or unacceptable permissions and information for their category.

[Back to Top]

What are the different device access categories that App Assist monitors? App Assist allows you to review your apps by device access type or by app category (Games, Social Networking Apps, Navigation Apps, or Other Apps).

The access types are as follows:

  1. Personal Info – Apps in this category can access your personal information, including your phone number, your contacts, and your calendar.
  2. Messages or Pictures – Apps in this category can access your text messages, emails, camera, and microphone.
  3. Location – Apps in this category can check your phone location.
  4. Battery – Apps in this category have high resource usage.

How do I fix battery drain? The Asurion LABS Application performs periodic backups of your contacts and phone location. Reducing the frequency of these backup can extend the battery life of your phone:

  1. See [FAQ: "How do I customize my sync settings?"] to see how to customize your auto backup/location schedule.

You can also turn off other features to extend the life of your battery:

  1. Turn off Wi-Fi when not being used. When on, the Wi-Fi uses the battery to run in the background and scan for networks.
  2. Turn off the GPS locator when not being used. When on, the GPS uses battery to ping satellites and keep your position updated. (Note that Asurion LABS Application will not work when the GPS locator is turned off.)
  3. Turn off Bluetooth when not being used. When on, even if you're not using a Bluetooth phone, it uses batter to send the Bluetooth wireless connection.
  4. Properly close out of applications. If not, programs continue to run in the background and drain the battery. If your phone doesn't seem to have a proper way to close applications, then look on the respective App Store for an app that will do it for you.
  5. Adjust the screen brightness to a lower level. The more you touch a touch screen, the more it stays lit up.
  6. Increase email push intervals to a longer time, or set to manual. Most phones have email intervals set to check for new emails every 15 minutes. If you set this interval to check less frequently, or set it to manual so that it checks only when you tell it to, will be less stress on the battery.
  7. Do not keep your phone hooked up to a charger when it's on a full charge. Doing so will reduce the life expectancy of the battery because in a sense it "trains" the phone to expect a constant power source (charger) instead of using its own battery.
  8. Try to at least once a month fully discharge your battery (drain it down to < 5%), and then full recharge it.
  9. Keep your phone out of direct sunlight. If the phone is being charged in a car or by a window, that can keep the battery hot and reduce its effectiveness and longevity.

My phone is running slowly. How can I fix that?

Try the following steps:

  1. When your phone is running slowly, turn your phone off, wait at least 30 seconds, and then turn it back on.
  2. If your phone is still running slowly, pull the battery out, wait at least 30 seconds, and then reinsert the battery. (It is safe to pull the battery out while the phone is on.)

[Back to Top]

My phone/apps are freezing. How can I fix that?

Try the following steps:

  1. When applications freeze, a quick fix is to turn your phone off, wait at least 30 seconds, and then turn it back on.
  2. If your phone does not respond to button presses or screen taps, pull the battery out, wait at least 30 seconds, and then reinsert the battery. (It is safe to pull the battery out while the phone is on.)
